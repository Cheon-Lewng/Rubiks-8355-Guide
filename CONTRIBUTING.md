# Contributing Guide
## Child-Friendly Rubik’s Cube Guide (8355 Method)

---

## Non-Negotiable Principle

> **This guide is written for children first.**

Adult clarity must never come at the cost of child understanding.

---

## Audience

### ✅ Write For
- children ages ~3–10
- absolute beginners

### ❌ Do NOT Write For
- speedcubers
- notation-first learners
- theory-focused readers

---

## Language Rules (Strict)

### ✅ Allowed in Kid Text
- short sentences
- concrete words
- physical actions

### 🚫 Forbidden in Kid Text
(Adult notes only)
- algorithm
- permutation
- orientation
- clockwise / counterclockwise

---

## Canonical Metaphors (Enforced)

Do NOT:
- invent new metaphors
- mix metaphors
- stretch metaphor meaning

Butterfly, Fish, Net, Sun, Petals, Working Door are **locked**.

---

## The Working Door (Strict)

- Exists in every step
- Always the same physical location
- Must never be solved early
- Its function may change; its place may not

Do NOT rename or replace this concept.

---

## Move Rules

### One Algorithm Rule
Only one move pattern exists:
- Catch the Butterfly / Catch the Fish

Do NOT:
- introduce new algorithms
- allow counting-based fixes

---

## Step-Specific Enforcement

### Step 2
- Only edges matching solved white corners may be selected
- Working Door may be front-left or front-right
- Butterfly insertion still allowed

---

### Step 3
- Solving is **relative**, not algorithmic
- Butterflies may escape but are not “caught”
- Temporary disorder is expected and safe
- Anchor concept is **adult-only**

Forbidden:
- fixed color maps
- counting cycles
- algorithmic phrasing

Contributors must not convert Step 3 into a procedural algorithm,
even if it appears simplifiable.

---

### Step 4
- Fish appear only here
- Repetition is intentional (the Dance)
- The Working Door is always front-right
- Right face is always the net
- Net goes **down first**

Final two twisted corners:
- are a known valid state
- require the single cube rotation
- must not be “fixed” any other way

Contributors must include reassurance that visual disorder during the dance is expected and safe.


___
### Cube Rotation Enforcement

Contributors must distinguish between:

- **horizontal cube rotation** (allowed for changing perspective and the Working Door),
- and **vertical or flipping rotations** (disallowed outside the final ceremonial turn in Step 4).

The prohibition on cube rotation exists to preserve **yellow‑up / white‑down stability**, not to prevent children from rotating the cube to look at different sides.

Recovery mechanics introduced in Step 1A (such as turning the bottom two layers together) must not be repurposed as solving strategies in later steps.

---

## Adult Notes

Adult notes must:
- be clearly labeled
- be visually separated
- never be required to complete a step

---

## Contributor Checklist

Before submitting, confirm:

- [ ] Yellow up until final step
- [ ] Canonical metaphors only
- [ ] Working Door preserved
- [ ] One algorithm only
- [ ] No blind repetition
- [ ] Emotional safety maintained

---

## Thank You ❤️

This guide teaches more than a cube.
It teaches:

- patience
- recovery
- confidence

That is the real puzzle we are solving.
