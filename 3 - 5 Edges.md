## STEP 3 – LAST EDGES

### 🎯 Big Goal
Help **all the edges** find their places.  
Make a **yellow plus** under the Sun.

This step is about **watching and noticing**.

---

### 📖 The Story (no moves)
The **Sun** is still up high.
It keeps shining.

Most friends are already home.
A few **edges** are still traveling.

They learn by watching one quiet friend.
Other edges move **around** that friend.

Sometimes the cube looks mixed.
That is normal.
The cube knows how to come back.

---

### 👀 What to Look For
- **Yellow edges** on the cube.
- One yellow edge that already looks happy.
- The **Working Door**, the open place where edges can trade spots.

You may see **butterflies** in the sky.
That only means an edge flew up to make space.
That is okay.

---

### 🧱 Rules for This Step
- We solve **edges only**.
- The **Working Door** helps edges move.
- Butterflies may **escape**, but **we do not catch** them.
- We do **not** use Catch the Butterfly to solve.
- No counting.
- No memorizing.
- Looking comes first.

---
### ✋ The Moves (story‑based, physical actions)
This step is quiet and thoughtful.  
There is no rush.

---

#### 1. Hold the Sun and the Door
Keep the **Sun** on top.  
The Sun stays there.

Find the **Working Door**.  
That door is always the same spot.

The **net** may come from the left **or** the right.  
We choose the side that lets yellow **look at us**.

---

#### 2. Find or Make a Yellow Friend
We want a **yellow edge** in the Working Door.

If the Working Door has a different piece:

- Let it leave using the **familiar escape move**.
- We are making space, not fixing.

When a **yellow edge** is in the Working Door and looking at you:

- This edge has **two good jobs**:
    - go into its home
    - **become the quiet friend**

These are the _only_ goals.

___
#### 3. Choose the Quiet Friend
Look under the Sun.

- If a yellow edge is already happy and correct up there:
    - That one may stay the **quiet friend**.
- If not:
    - The yellow edge in the Working Door **becomes** the quiet friend.

Only one quiet friend is needed.

___
#### 4. Try to Send the Friend Home
Now we experiment.

- Gently turn the **top** so the yellow edge in the Working Door can try for its home.
- Bring the **net up**.

Watch closely.
When you bring the **net up**, one edge gets gently pushed out of a home.

This is not a problem.  
This is the cube talking to you.

That edge is the one we try to help **next**.

___
#### 5. Try to Help the Pushed‑Out Edge
Look at the edge that was pushed out.

What it wants is simple:
- it wants to go **to its home**

So we try this:
- Gently turn the **top** so _that edge’s home_ is ready.
- Bring the **net down**.

Sometimes this works right away.  
That feels nice.

---
#### 6. When It Cannot Go Home (Very Normal)
Sometimes the edge **cannot** go home yet.

This can happen because:
- it is facing a hard way
- or its home is the Working Door
- or the cube is not ready yet

When this happens:
- we do **not** force it
- we do **not** fix it

Instead, we do something gentle and safe:
👉 **Place the edge in any unsolved spot on the top.**

That is all.

Putting it on top keeps it safe and visible.  
It can wait there.

---
#### 7. Keep Going From What You See
Now:
- look again at the Working Door
- notice who is quiet
- notice who moved last

Each time the net moves:
- one edge comes out
- that edge is the next one you _try_ to help

Trying is enough.

___
#### 8. Notice a Special “Stuck Pattern”

Sometimes you will see this:

- You put a yellow edge into its home.
- Another yellow edge drops out somewhere else.
- When you fix that one, the first one falls back out.

It can feel like the cube is undoing you.

This is **not** a mistake.

This means something important:

👉 **The yellow edge in the Working Door needs to stop traveling  
and become the new quiet friend.**
It does this by gently trading places with one of its yellow friends, so it can stay still while a different edge travels next.

---
#### Gentle Reassurance (Keep This Tone)

Sometimes helping an edge means:
- putting it straight home  
    Sometimes it means:
- giving it a safe place to wait

Both are good moves.

This step works by **watching**, not by knowing.

---

### 🔁 If Something Looks Weird
That’s expected.

If the yellow plus looks broken:
- It can return.
- Nothing is ruined.

If corners or edges look messy:
- Pause.
- Pick a new edge to build around.
- Watch again.

This step is safe.
The cube always survives.

---

### ✅ Check When You’re Done
You are done when:
- **All edges** are solved.
- A **yellow plus** sits under the Sun.
- You stopped by **looking**, not counting.

Take a breath.
You did careful work.

---

### 🧠 Adult Notes (Optional)

- Step 3 is intentionally non‑algorithmic.
- One yellow edge functions as an anchor (quiet friend); others are solved relative to it.
- The quiet friend may change when progress loops — this is expected.
- The Working Door is used to cycle edges; there is no direct insertion.
- Butterflies may escape to create space, but are never caught or solved here.
- Parking a yellow edge on the top layer is intentional when it arrives with yellow on the side; placing it on top preserves it safely until it can be solved relative to the current quiet friend.
- Temporary disorder is expected; the yellow cross may appear broken and then recover.
- Repetition is guided by visible improvement, never by counting or fixed color orders.
