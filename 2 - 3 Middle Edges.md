## STEP 2 – MIDDLE EDGES

### 🎯 Big Goal
Help **three middle friends** find their homes.  
One door stays open on purpose.

---

### 📖 The Story (no moves)
The cube has a **Working Door**.  
This door is special.  
It stays open so friends can go in and out safely.

Some friends live in the **middle** of the cube.  
They are not white.  
They are not yellow.

Today, we help **three** of them move in.  
Not four.  
Three.

The **sun** stays up in the sky.  
The door stays right where it is.  
Nothing is rushed.

---

### 👀 What to Look For
- Find a **butterfly** in the sky (the top layer).
- This butterfly has **two colors**.
- Those two colors **match a white corner that is already done**.
- That butterfly has a home next to that corner.

If the colors match, it’s the right friend.  
If not, we leave it alone.

---

### 🧱 Rules for This Step
- The **Working Door stays unsolved** on purpose.
- The **net** is the layer with the Working Door.
- The net can be on the **right or the left**.

- We only help butterflies **from the top**.
- We only choose butterflies that **match a done white corner**.

- We may move the **top two layers together** to line the butterfly's **home** up with the open Working Door space.

- Before you begin, look at the butterfly’s **side color**.
- Hold the cube so the **net layer** shows that color.

- We help **three** middle friends. No more.

- Releasing a friend and trying again is **safe**.

---

### ✋ The Moves (story‑based, physical actions)
1. Hold the cube so the **Working Door is in front**.
2. Look in the sky for a **butterfly whose two colors match a white corner that is already done**.
3. Gently turn the **top two layers together** until the butterfly’s **home** lines up with the door.
4. Make sure the **net layer** (the layer with the Working Door) is the one that **shows the butterfly’s side color**.
	- The butterfly is still in the sky, now sitting against its matching wall.
5. **Catch the Butterfly**.
6. Smile. One middle friend is home.
7. Find another matching butterfly.
8. Stop when **three** middle friends are home.

---

### 🔁 If Something Looks Weird
That’s okay.

If a butterfly lands the wrong way:
- **Lift the net**.
- Let the butterfly **escape back to the sky**.
- Line it up again.
- Catch it again.

No guessing.  
No rushing.  
The door stays open.

---

### ✅ Check When You’re Done
You are done when:
- **Three** middle friends are in place.
- **One** middle spot is still empty.
- The **Working Door is still open**.
- The **sun is still up**.

If four are done, open the door again and let one out.  
That’s not a mistake.  
That’s part of the game.

---

### 🧠 Adult Notes (Optional)
- This step inserts **exactly three middle edges**.
- Only edges matching **already solved white corners** are eligible.
- The Working Door may be **front‑right or front‑left**; the **net is always that layer**.
- Butterflies are **top‑layer pieces** and are solved via **Catch the Butterfly**.
- The top two layers may rotate together **only to align** the target butterfly with the door.
- Incorrect insertions are fixed by **lifting the net to eject**—no special cases.
- Do **not** solve the fourth middle edge. Keep the Working Door unsolved for later steps.
- Emphasize safety, release, and retry. Avoid counting or repetition “until it works.”
